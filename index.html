    We are PARC training

    Promoting Anti-discriminatory Responses for Change in the mental health field. If you are interested in learning more about diverse and inclusive practice get in touch with us today.

    our expertise

    training

    We provide bespoke training to mental health organisations and professional bodies on anti-discriminatory practice, exploring the inequalities in mental health in terms of the nine protected characteristics and the social graces. We challenge current practices, and encourage reflection on how we position ourselves as Mental Health Professionals in order to meet the needs of our diverse communities in the therapeutic and supervisory realm.

    Topics covered:

  • Diversity in an Irish context
  • Unconscious bias
  • Triple Barrier Affect in mental health
  • Cultural Competence vs Cultural Humility
  • Working with interpreters
  • Exploring Theoretical perspectives
  • The Danger of the Single Story
  • Multicultural supervision

    • consultancy

    We work with mental health organisations and individual practitioners on how to make their practice more accessible to different groups and how to attract a more diverse workforce. The use of case studies, organisational reflection and strategizing are utilised.

    supervision

    We provide individual and group supervision to mental health professionals and supervisors who are interested in developing/enhancing their practice in working with diversity.

    our vision

    Mental health professionals in Ireland would be mindful and aware of the effects of social injustice and discrimination on mental health and modify their practice in order to respond to the unique needs of all of their clients.

    our mission

    To raise awareness and depth of understanding in Mental Health Professionals on the impact of conscious and unconscious bias, discrimination and prejudice on members of diverse groups and develop an inclusive practice in order to provide a meaningful experience for service users.

    This will be accomplished through training, supervision, reflective learning, consultation and ongoing support building competency, confidence, and skills in Mental Health Professionals.
